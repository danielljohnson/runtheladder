Why did I start this program?
The tech industry's full of career coaches and interview prep, but they miss a key gap: most people lack baseline career fitness for big moves, scrambling last-minute when change hits. I've been there too and designed this program for myself as much as anyone.
What does this have to do with running?
Runners know you can't prep for a race the day before, it takes months of steady training to peak, and most athletes maintain baseline fitness year-round to make it easier. That concept's missing in careers.
How does the program work?
Run the Ladder sends daily email reps to sharpen your skills and prep for your next move, paired with a private Slack group for discussion and networking. Weekly themes build long-term career fitness. Live expert sessions, led by me and other tech leaders, keep you on track.
Why 6 weeks?
Habits take time to stick. Most people happily spend an hour daily on physical fitness but next to nothing on their career. Give 15 minutes a day for 6 weeks, and you'll build lasting career fitness and confidence.
How does this compare to LeetCode prep or resume writing services?
LeetCode and resume services are last-minute fixes, great for coding tests or polishing a pitch, but they don't build career fitness. LeetCode's a necessary evil with plenty of practice out there. Resumes are one way to tell your career story; this program shapes it step-by-step over 6 weeks.
